Preview: Building Climate-Friendly Homes in Disaster-Prone Areas

Published on: October 12, 2024

The increasing frequency of extreme weather events, like Cyclone Idai, has highlighted the urgent need for climate-friendly housing solutions, particularly in vulnerable regions such as Chimanimani and Beitbridge. Many homes in these areas, built with traditional materials, have proven inadequate in the face of natural disasters, leading to widespread loss of life, livestock, and shelter. This blog explores the importance of designing resilient, eco-friendly structures that can withstand harsh weather conditions while remaining affordable for rural communities. It also discusses potential strategies for lowering construction costs, including the use of recycled materials and community-driven building techniques. Discover how climate-smart architecture can save lives, protect livelihoods, and create sustainable homes in disaster-prone areas..
